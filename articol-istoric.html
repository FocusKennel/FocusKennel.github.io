Fondatorul · Focus Kennel · București

Dr. Cristian Dragomir

Medic veterinar · Crescător de Dobermann · Fondator Focus KennelBiografie

After I saw for the first time a Dobermann, I knew this was the dog I wanted to own. The Dobermann impresses me by his athletic structure, firm expression, joyfulness, his great capacity to work and make a team with his owner. In 1980 I got my first Dobermann — a passion that has never left me since.

Education & Early Career

In 1990, Dr. Cristian Dragomir graduated from the Faculty of Veterinary Medicine, Bucharest. Following graduation, he completed an internship at the small animals clinic of Dr. Michel Majoube in Sablé, France — an early introduction to Western European standards of veterinary practice.

1993–2007 — Together with Dr. Horia Elefterescu and Dr. Ilinca Cioculescu, he co-founded „Clinica Veterinară Armeneasca 34" — the first non-stop (24/7) veterinary clinic in Bucharest.

1994 — The Beginning of Focus Kennel

In 1994, after years of working with and studying the breed, Dr. Dragomir decided to breed Dobermanns. He imported two females with excellent bloodlines: Doriental Loren and Blackness Bessy — daughter of Jaron Royal Bell and granddaughter of Tor Bay de Amiluc. This was the beginning of Focus Kennel.

1997 — Graaf Tila raises the bar

In 1997, he imported a beautiful female puppy, Vana Fortobella, and a three-year-old male: Graaf Tila v. Neerlands Stam.

Tila proved to be an exceptional work dog — although training started quite late. He passed the BH exam with 59 points out of 60 and scored Excellent at the SchH I exam with 271 points, including a perfect 100 for the attack phase. Tila was also a very successful show dog, winning in Romania, Yugoslavia, Croatia, Hungary, Greece, Bulgaria and Ukraine.

Graaf Tila v. Neerlands Stam — Mid and East European Champion 1999 · 2nd in Champion Class at the European Dog Show, Poznań 2000.

His offspring — Focus Olla, Focus Orson, Focus Ozzy, Focus Ono, Focus Cardinal, Focus Castor, Focus Born to be Wild, Focus Bohemian Rhapsody — collected many Rpj, CAC, CACIB titles, and several became Romanian Champions.

1998–2003 — „Câinele Meu"

Between 1998 and 2003, Dr. Dragomir was the founder and owner of „Câinele Meu" (My Dog), a widely appreciated Romanian canine magazine. He authored numerous articles and coordinated the veterinary medicine section. He was also a regular guest on radio and television programmes dedicated to animal care.

2001 — Dobermann Club Romania

In 2001, Dr. Dragomir co-founded the DCR — Dobermann Club Romania and served as its President until 2005. The same year, his friend George Bundan brought to Romania an excellent show female, Tom-Dober Elli, who joined the Focus Kennel breeding programme.

Other important studs used for the kennel's line during this period: Icaro di Villa Castelli, Baron Nike Renewal, Velenberg Julfil Dastin, Axel di Casa Ravasi, Casanova Altobello, Tamerlan iz Slavnoi Stai, and Gino Gomez del Citone. Notable offspring: Focus Sonya (Romanian Junior Champion, Derby Winner 2000) and Focus Honeymoon (Derby Winner 2002).

2007–2015 — Surgery & International Practice

2007–2008 — Works in Greece at Vet Act clinic, Athens, as a surgeon.

From July 2008 until 2015, Dr. Dragomir served as Medical Director of Marco Vet Clinical Veterinary Hospital, Bucharest — the hospital's principal surgeon. His clinical focus: soft tissue surgery, orthopaedics, abdominal ultrasound and radiology.

Between 2008 and 2011 he collaborated closely with an Italian medical team at Marco Vet: Dr. Patricia Knafelz, Dr. Alessandro Floris and Dr. Massimo Floris.

2009 — CDMEC

In 2009, Dr. Dragomir founded CDMEC — Centrala pentru Diagnosticul Maladiilor Ereditare ale Câinelui (Central for the Diagnosis of Hereditary Diseases of the Dog). From 2013 he has served as Vice President of CDMEC.

2011 — Padova Certification

In 2011, Dr. Dragomir completed the Ce.Le.Ma.Sche course in Padova, Italy, and became an accredited practitioner of the Italian Central — beginning a long-standing collaboration with Italian veterinary partners.

2013 — FCI Accreditation, Dortmund

In 2013 in Dortmund, Dr. Dragomir received accreditation as an Official FCI Lecturer for Skeletal Diseases from GRSK — Gesellschaft für Röntgendiagnostik genetisch beeinflusster Skeletterkrankungen bei Kleintieren e.V. He is also a founding member of the Societatea Română Veterinară de Ortopedie (SRVO).

Throughout his career, Dr. Dragomir has participated in numerous congresses, courses and workshops in Romania and abroad, and has visited leading veterinary clinics and hospitals in Germany, Italy and France.

Philosophy

In my opinion a good Dobermann should be more than just an anatomically correct show dog. He must be, first of all, well balanced and sure in his responses — a reliable guardian and a lovable family dog.

The passion for Dobermanns made me travel a lot and make friends all over Europe. My hobby also brought me close to some very special people who share my love for this breed and work together with me.

„A good Dobermann should be well balanced and sure in his responses — a reliable guardian and a lovable family dog."
— Dr. Cristian Dragomir
